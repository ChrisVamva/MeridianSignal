### 🥇 Option 1: OpenAI Codex ($20/mo) — The Complex Codebase Champion

*   **Quality & Efficiency:** Codex is purpose-built for complex work. Unlike a simple chatbot, it operates as an autonomous engineering agent that can understand your entire codebase, plan and execute multi-step tasks, and even debug and test its own changes. One user reported that Codex completed 24 days worth of coding in just 12 hours, showcasing a 16x productivity boost for large-scale tasks.
*   **Raw Privileges:** The $20 ChatGPT Plus plan includes a generous weekly usage cap that, while not infinite, is designed for "deep sessions". A single, 68-minute debug session might consume around 20% of a weekly quota, indicating the allowance is substantial enough for significant, multi-hour projects before hitting limits.

### 🥈 Option 2: Ollama Cloud (GLM/Devstral) ($20/mo) — The GPU Powerhouse

*   **Quality & Efficiency:** Ollama Cloud offers access to some of the most powerful open-source models like GLM and Devstral. If your work involves heavy GPU-bound tasks (like model fine-tuning or massive data processing), this is a strong contender. However, its efficiency for complex codebases is more situational. You'll get the most value from targeted, intensive sessions rather than continuous background work.
*   **Raw Privileges:** This is the key differentiator. Ollama measures usage in **GPU time**, not tokens. This means there's no fixed token limit. The Pro plan offers "50x more cloud usage than Free". Your effective token allowance can be very high if you use smaller, faster models, or lower if you frequently use massive models like a 480B-parameter powerhouse.

### 🥉 Option 3: Google Antigravity ($20/mo) — The Speedy Integrator

*   **Quality & Efficiency:** Antigravity is a full-fledged IDE powered by Google's Gemini models. Its strength lies in deep integration with Google's ecosystem and a focus on multi-agent orchestration, which can be a huge advantage for projects involving browser automation or complex, cross-platform tasks. It is particularly strong at managing large projects without slowdowns.
*   **Raw Privileges:** The Pro plan offers a "high, generous quota" that refreshes every 5 hours. While Google has been criticized for not providing exact public token limits, user reports and official updates confirm the Pro tier provides a significant, usable volume for daily professional work, with limits that are far more generous than the free tier.

### Option 4: Anthropic Claude Code ($20/mo) — The Lightweight Specialist

*   **Quality & Efficiency:** Claude Code is a capable assistant for short coding tasks, debugging, and working on smaller codebases. It's excellent for quick fixes and sprint-like development, but its design makes it less suitable for the long, complex refactoring sessions you're interested in.
*   **Raw Privileges:** This is Claude Code's biggest weakness. The $20 Pro plan is highly restrictive, with limits of approximately **45 messages every 5 hours** and a weekly cap of **40-80 hours** of usage. These limits are a significant bottleneck for serious development work and are the reason many professional developers find they need to upgrade to the $100 or $200 plans.

If your main goal is maximum efficiency for managing complex codebases, **OpenAI Codex** is the clear winner. It's the only $20 tool specifically designed for the kind of autonomous, multi-hour, deep-dive work you're describing.
