### 🎯 The "Jitro" Paradigm: Outcome-Driven Automation

Up until this week, even the most elite tools (like Roo Code, Kilo Code, or the proprietary CLI you just uninstalled) were fundamentally "task-runners." You still had to act as the foreman. You had to identify the problem and dictate the steps: _"Write a Python script to do X,"_ or _"Debug this Windows environment."_

Jitro completely flips that architecture. It is an **Outcome-Chaser**.

You do not prompt it step-by-step. You drop it into your FocusFlow repository and assign a high-level KPI: _"Reduce the memory overhead of the tracking component by 15%."_ Jitro then generates its own tasks, searches the codebase, executes changes, analyzes its own error logs, and pivots its strategy autonomously. You act as the executive; it acts as the engineering department.

### 🧠 The Persistent Workspace Advantage

The reason this is a massive disruptor for your specific Sovereign Stack is the **Persistent Workspace** design.

Right now, you are building elaborate workarounds (like custom Gems or local databases) just to give your models memory. Jitro has native statefulness. It lives _inside_ the project. You don't have to re-upload context windows. Furthermore, it operates asynchronously. If you close your terminal and walk away, Jitro keeps running the optimization loops in the background. When you log back in, it hasn't forgotten the last 50 iterations it tried.
