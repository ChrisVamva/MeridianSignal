# Knowledge Architecture Hub

## Definition

Knowledge architecture is the part of the archive concerned with how ideas are structured, linked, compressed, symbolized, and turned into a reasoning substrate.

## Why This Area Is Distinctive

This is one of the most original parts of the archive.
It contains attempts to think about:

- semantic compression
- symbolic systems
- structured extraction
- synthesis across domains
- the relationship between concept, language, and reasoning

## Preservation Rule

Preserve the parts that:

- help structure thinking
- make synthesis more legible
- create reusable symbolic or conceptual methods

Discard the parts that:

- rely only on grand language
- lack operational or conceptual clarity
- never resolve into a framework that can actually be reused
