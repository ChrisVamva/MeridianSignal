# Structured Insight Extraction

## Purpose

Structured extraction is the practice of forcing a text, paper, tool briefing, or research subject into a small number of disciplined fields.

## Why It Matters

Without structure, research notes tend to become either:

- summaries that are too vague
- or excerpts that are too raw

Structured extraction is a bridge between those extremes.

## Recommended Fields

For many subjects, the most useful fields are:

- concept
- tension
- why it matters
- relation to the wider system
- operational consequence
- open question

## XML And Similar Schemes

The archive contains explicit interest in XML-style tagging for insight extraction.
That survives not because XML itself is sacred, but because tagging disciplines attention.

## Practical Rule

Use structure when it improves retrieval and synthesis.
Do not let the structure become decorative overhead.
