# Research Workflow

## Core Workflow

The archive suggests a recurring research loop that is worth preserving explicitly.

1. Detect a promising subject.
2. Gather candidate material.
3. Compare tools, frameworks, or interpretations around it.
4. Extract the operational and conceptual signal.
5. Connect it to the larger system.
6. Decide whether it belongs in projects, foundations, or strategic filters.

## What Makes A Subject Worth Pursuing

A subject becomes worth deeper effort when it satisfies at least one of these:

- it improves long-term research capability
- it expands agentic or tool leverage
- it deepens a conceptual framework you revisit often
- it closes a practical gap in an active project

## Strong Output Formats

The archive is strongest when research ends in one of these formats:

- a distilled brief
- a project lens
- a subject hub
- a strategic warning
- a framework note that clarifies a recurring concept

## Weak Output Formats

The archive is weakest when research ends as:

- raw enthusiasm
- isolated fragments without context
- duplicated snapshots of the same tool landscape
- abstract praise with no judgment

## Recommended Routine

For lasting quality, each serious investigation should end with:

- one short summary of the subject
- one explicit reason it matters
- one decision about whether it survives
- links to the most relevant adjacent subjects
