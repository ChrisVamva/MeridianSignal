# Research Identity Sketch

## Status

This is a provisional sketch, not a final doctrine.

Its job is to give an agent enough orientation to work usefully without pretending that your research identity is fully settled.

## Core Orientation

The research is centered on building a personal research-and-action system around:

- agentic systems
- tool intelligence
- workflow design
- knowledge architecture
- sovereignty and local control
- the translation of research into living projects

## Recurring Ambitions

### Build systems, not isolated notes

The archive repeatedly pushes toward systems that can:

- remember
- route work
- compress knowledge
- assist research
- operate across time rather than in one-off sessions

### Prefer structure over accumulation

There is a clear bias toward:

- explicit architecture
- compressed summaries
- comparative judgment
- reusable frameworks

and against:

- raw accumulation
- vague excitement
- ornamental complexity

### Seek useful novelty

The research does not merely want to understand what exists.
It wants to reach the next useful configuration:

- the next project form
- the next workflow refinement
- the next agent architecture
- the next meaningful combination of ideas

## Research Temperament

The work tends to favor:

- skepticism toward hype
- interest in structural shifts
- strong attraction to sovereignty, modularity, and replaceability
- comfort with crossing from technical detail into larger conceptual frames

## Practical Reading For Agents

When engaging new material, assume:

- the user cares more about durable implications than surface novelty
- architecture matters more than marketing
- comparison should lead to a verdict
- summaries should clarify what changes next

## Open Aspect

This identity layer should remain flexible.
It should be revised as the research matures rather than treated as a frozen self-description.
